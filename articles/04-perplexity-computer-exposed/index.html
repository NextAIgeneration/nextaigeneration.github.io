#04 — Perplexity "Computer": The Full Exposure

13 March 2026 ALERT GDPR PATTERN

On March 13, 2026, Perplexity AI (115 Sansome St, Suite 900, San Francisco, CA 94104) activated "Computer" on our European company without consent. Not a feature update. Not a beta. A full cloud orchestrator with access to 400+ enterprise applications, deployed on every account simultaneously. Opt-out, not opt-in.

We documented everything. This is the full exposure.

What is Perplexity "Computer"?

Their own marketing says it plainly:

"Chat answers. Agents do tasks. Computer works."

Perplexity Computer is a cloud orchestrator that:

  • Routes queries across 20 frontier AI models — GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, and others. Perplexity owns none of them.
  • Connects to 400+ enterprise applications — Salesforce, Notion, Google Analytics, Snowflake, Slack, Gmail, Outlook, GitHub, HubSpot, Databricks, and more.
  • Deploys autonomous sub-agents that browse, research, create content, and connect your tools in the background — without you being present.
  • Persists context across sessions — "It remembers past context across sessions." Their words, not ours.
  • Integrates with Slack where a simple @Computer in any channel gives Perplexity access to your conversations.

"Personal Computer": 24/7 Access to Your Machine

In parallel, Perplexity launched "Personal Computer": software that runs 24/7 on a dedicated Mac mini, giving their cloud platform persistent access to every file, application, and session on the machine.

CEO Aravind Srinivas describes it as:

"A digital proxy for you, working constantly on your behalf."

Read that again. A San Francisco company installs a permanent proxy on your hardware that works "constantly" — meaning it never stops collecting, processing, and sending data to their cloud.

The Activation: Mass Opt-Out on European Companies

On March 13, 2026, every Perplexity Enterprise and Max account received an email announcing Computer was "live for all users". The email invited us to "manage permissions" and "set default preferences for new connectors."

The wording is revealing:

"Set your default preference for new connectors as new connectors will be available according to your preferences."

Translation: New connectors activate by default. It is up to the company to disable them. One by one. Out of 400+.

This is not opt-in. This is mass opt-out. On a Belgian company. In Europe. Under GDPR.

The Workshop: "Beyond Prompts"

Four days after forcing activation on every account, Perplexity scheduled a webinar for March 17, 2026: "Beyond prompts: How Perplexity Computer executes entire projects."

The sequence is deliberate:

  1. March 13 — Activate Computer on all accounts (opt-out)
  2. March 17 — Host a workshop teaching people how to use what was already activated

They don't ask if you want it. They turn it on, then teach you to depend on it. The first hit is free. The workshop is the onboarding.

Enterprise Growth Lead Janneta Tabakov will present Computer as a "general-purpose digital worker." The event is organized by Jenny Sung, Perplexity AI, Inc. — registered company number 118769127.

Already Caught: Amazon Court Injunction (March 9, 2026)

Four days before activating Computer on our European company, Perplexity was hit with a federal court injunction in the United States.

On March 9-10, 2026, Judge Maxine Chesney of the US District Court, Northern District of California, granted Amazon a preliminary injunction against Perplexity's Comet browser.

What Perplexity did:

• Comet browser posed as human traffic (disguised as Chrome) rather than identifying as an AI agent

• Made automated purchases on Amazon on behalf of users

• Accessed password-protected customer accounts without Amazon's authorization

• Transmitted users' private account information to Perplexity's servers

The judge found Amazon "is likely to succeed on the merits" with "strong evidence" supporting fraud allegations. Amazon had sent five cease-and-desist requests before filing suit on October 31, 2025. All five were ignored.

Legal violations cited: Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (federal), Comprehensive Computer Data Access and Fraud Act (state), breach of Amazon's terms prohibiting automated data gathering.

Perplexity's defense? They argued purchases were legitimate because "users authorized their AI agents." The same logic they use with Computer: you didn't opt out, so you opted in.

The SOC 2 Shield

Perplexity claims Computer is "built on secure infrastructure that is SOC 2 Type II certified."

SOC 2 Type II certifies that Perplexity's own infrastructure follows security controls. It says nothing about the 20 third-party models your data is routed to. It says nothing about the 400+ connectors accessing your enterprise tools. It says nothing about GDPR compliance.

SOC 2 is an American auditing standard. It is not a European data protection certification. Using it as a trust signal for European companies is misleading at best.
